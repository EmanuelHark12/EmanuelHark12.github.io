Exploration Data Analysis of Restaurants in Ifood

Analysis of data from restaurants around the homes of IME Jr members

11/16/2021

Introduction

This is an analysis of data on Ifood's restaurants that are located around the homes of the IME Jr's members. The main objective of this analysis is to collect data from the internet through web-scraping and process them.

For this analysis, the R language was used with the packages:

  • Stringr: String-type variable treatments
  • Tidyr: Tidy Data
  • Dplyr: Data manipulation to extract results
  • Purrr: Functional programming
  • Forcats: Treatment of categorical variables
  • Ggplot2: Elaboration of statistical graphics
  • Parsnip: Simplifying Model Building
  • Recipes: Data preparation through feature engineering for model building
  • Broom: Treatment of statistical results
  • Workflows: Integration between parsnip packages and recipes

Treatment

Por conta de praticidade, cada membro da EJ utilizou a extensão Web Scraper - Free Web Scraping em navegadores Chromium para a coleta das seguintes informações referentes a 30 empresas localizadas ao redor do membro: For a pratice reason, each IME JR member use a Chromium extension 'Web Scraper - Free Web Scraping' for collect the followings information about 30 restaurants around the house of IME Jr member:

  • id: Variable of identity;
  • Name: Name of restaurant;
  • Type: Specialty of restaurant;
  • Time: Duration of delivery from restaurant to home in minutes;
  • Price: Price of restaurant food or drink in BRL;
  • Rating: Rating given by user to the restaurant;
  • User: IME Jr member who collect restaurant information;

Exploration Analysis

For analysis, we used data from myself and 3 other IME Jr members, cited by E, M, A and G.

From now on, the variables will be analyzed one-dimensionally.

The first variable observed is the Type, where there is a predominance of Snacks, which is the most common restaurant specialty, then there are Japanese, which grew recently, and Pizza type restaurants, which have more than 10 restaurants.

The next variable is Time, which has been broken down into intervals of 15 minutes in length and we see that most restaurants are at intervals of 30 minutes to 1 hour.

For a Price variable, which has been divided into ranges with a length of 5 BRL and see what the price range of most restaurants is [5.10], followed by the range of (0.5] BRL, with a range [-1, 0) indicates that the restaurant has not set the price of your product.

For a Rating variable, which was divided into intervals with a length of 1 and we see that the price range of most restaurants is (4.5], there are several restaurants with a rating of 0 due to the recent entry into the app.

In this part, we'll analyze the relation between the four variables used with IME Jr's members(User).

With a variable Type, perceived that G member has the more diversity of type of restaurants.

With a variable Price, perceived that overall price of E member has higher than others members of IME Jr because of your geography location.

With a variable Time, perceived that overall time of E member has lower than others members of IME Jr because of geography location, because E lives in center of São Paulo.

With the variable Rating, it was noticed that the variability of the ratings of member A is higher than that of the other members of IME Jr.

Conclusion

With these informations, see the restaurants of the center of São Paulo is more expensive and the food delivery are more faster than other cities, like A and G members or other district, like of M.
